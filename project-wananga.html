Virtual Immersive Wānanga

Role / Type
Designer & Researcher / Bicultural
Platform
VR / AR
Timeline
2022 - 2023
Location
Aotearoa New Zealand

Traditional educational spaces often lacked accessible digital alternatives that respected bicultural collaborative practices. To bridge this gap, I collaborated with Māori communities to design and develop a virtual learning and workshop space (Wānanga).

The Challenge

Creating a digital space that authentically reflects Māori values and Tikanga required deep community collaboration from the outset. The challenge was to avoid imposing Western design paradigms on an inherently bicultural context — ensuring the virtual environment felt safe, culturally grounded, and genuinely useful for cross-cultural learning.

Design Process & Methodology

I applied rigorous co-design methodologies to ensure the virtual environment authentically reflected Māori values and Tikanga, prototyping the space using VR/AR tools with a focus on inclusive design and active learning. Each design decision was reviewed in partnership with community members throughout the process.

Outcome

I successfully delivered a functional prototype of a virtual Wānanga that provided a culturally safe, immersive space for cross-cultural collaboration, demonstrating a strong commitment to inclusive and bicultural design.
