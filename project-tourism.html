Immersive Regenerative Tourism

Role / Type
Postdoctoral Researcher / UX
Platform
Mobile AR
Timeline
2025 - Present
Location
Aotearoa New Zealand

With the tourism industry in Aotearoa New Zealand requiring a transformative approach to foster culturally grounded experiences, I joined an NZD $8.2M MBIE-funded multi-university project as a Postdoctoral Researcher. My objective was to develop on-site AR experiences that enhance tourists' place attachment and collective memory.

The Challenge

Traditional tourism often treats cultural heritage as a passive backdrop. The challenge was to integrate digital spatial computing—specifically mobile AR—in a way that active engages users while strictly respecting Māori values such as kaitiakitanga (guardianship).

Design Process & Methodology

By leading the UX evaluation and co-design methodologies, I ensured the digital solutions were both engaging and culturally relevant. We conducted iterative testing across various physical environments to optimize the AR anchor stability and readability of spatial UI.

Outcome

I am currently contributing to this 5-year national research programme, effectively reshaping tourism by bridging cultural preservation with advanced spatial computing to deliver accessible digital heritage experiences.
